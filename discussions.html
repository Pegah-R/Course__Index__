Quesion:

1. A key part to generative design and code is pattern and routine. Akin to a chess player, marking each move down, and keeping a trace of your decisions can prove useful for your own process and future making. As a designer, to know that there might be a variety of possibilities to a given situation, can aid our ways of observing by using tools in novel ways. In one series of work, Van Rossum looks to fundamentals of waveforms as a departure point and reference point for connecting an interface to affecting the parameters of a letterform. If the letters explode, so too would the speakers. Find 1 unique example of a pattern in nature, that appears to be programmatic. Post a visual of this pattern and describe how it is programmatic in nature.

Answer:

One example in nature that has programmatic patterns is cacti, like many other plants and things. The way their spikes are formed and the way in which they grow is programmatic.

There are lines in which the spikes are located, that spiral toward the center. The spiral is like a Fibonacci sequence of numbers, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, which the two numbers before sum up the preceding number, that spiral together into the center. There are raised surfaces on which the spikes grow that also move and change with the spiral. The size of the spikes like the raised surface change and get smaller with the pattern spiraling in.

The plant's new leaves emerge from a spherical growth tip, which is made up of an outer shell and a mushy core. As the plant grows, the shell grows faster than the core, which causes the spiral ridges to form in the shell because of excess surface area, similar to wrinkles on our skin. The raised surfaces on the cactus then become places for the spikes. The perfect growth and the two different speed at which the parts grow creates this pattern or shape on the cactus.

Question:

(from the lecture) How can embodiment play a more critical role in my own design process?

Answer:

By understanding and paying attention to our emotions, feelings, and sensations, we can better convey and represent them, and even further, recognize what others are feeling and how we can represent them, so it appeals to others. In our design process like said before if we think about our experience and pay much attention to it, it reveals a lot about the work, the result, the mistakes, and the issues. Therefore embodiment should play a critical role in our design process. We should ask questions about our most basic interactions with tools and things we use and try to understand and look at them from a different perspective, and even do research on them. I think being and placing ourselves where we like and enjoy specifically in a natural setting can help our creativity and it can be inspiring as well, which is helpful to our own design process.
